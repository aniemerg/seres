## RAW RESOURCES

### 1. REGOLITH TYPES
- **Highland Regolith**: Dominated by anorthite (CaAl2Si2O8); enriched in Al (up to 33% by mass), Ca (up to 19% CaO); 20-24% SiO2, 3-4% Al2O3
- **Mare Regolith/Basalt**: 15-35% plagioclase feldspar, 40-65% pyroxene, 0-35% olivine, 0-25% ilmenite; high-Ti mare contains 10-20% ilmenite
- **Polar Regolith**: Contains water ice deposits; 5.6 ± 2.9% water concentration in Cabeus crater
- **Generic Regolith**: 42-45% oxygen by mass; ~60-80 µm grain size; depth: 4-5m in mare, 10-15m in highlands

**Elemental Composition (by percentage):**
- Oxygen: 42-45%
- Silicon: 20-23% (44.9% as SiO2)
- Aluminum: 7-9% (17.5% as Al2O3, up to 18% in anorthite)
- Iron: 6-10% (12.2% as FeO, 15% in mare basalt)
- Calcium: 8-10% (12.1% as CaO)
- Magnesium: 5-7% (8.8% as MgO)
- Titanium: 1-2% (2.5% as TiO2, up to 7% in high-Ti mare)
- Sodium: 0.5-0.8% (0.44% as Na2O)
- Potassium: 0.3-0.4% (0.11% as K2O, up to 0.8% in KREEP)
- Phosphorus: 0.05-0.2% (0.6% in KREEP)

### 2. MINERALS & ORES

**Primary Silicate Minerals:**
- **Anorthite (CaAl2Si2O8)**: Highland mineral; up to 19% CaO, 20-24% SiO2; source of Al, Si, Ca
- **Plagioclase Feldspar**: (Ca,Na)(Al2Si)4O8; anorthite-dominant; >90% of highland anorthosite
- **Olivine**: (Mg,Fe)2SiO4; forsterite (Mg2SiO4) to fayalite (Fe2SiO4); concentrated in mare soil; enriched in Cr
- **Pyroxene**: (Ca,Fe,Mg,Ti,Al)2Si2O6; includes augite, enstatite (MgSiO3), wollastonite (CaSiO3), ferrosilite (FeSiO3)
- **Orthoclase (KAlSi3O8)**: Source of K, Si, Al; potassium feldspar

**Oxide Minerals:**
- **Ilmenite (FeTiO3)**: 0-25% in basalt; high-Ti mare: 10-20%; volatile-enriched; contains 0.16-0.27% sulfur
- **Ulvospinel (Fe2TiO4)**: Reduces to rutile, iron, oxygen
- **Spinel (MgAl2O4)**: Also Fe,Mg)(Cr,Al,Fe,Ti)2O4; hardness 16 GPa; melting point 2135°C
- **Chromite (FeCr2O4)**: Chromium source
- **Hercynite (FeAl2O4)**: Iron-aluminum oxide
- **Magnetite (Fe3O4)**: From fayalite oxidation at 400-700°C; from Fe oxidation
- **Rutile (TiO2)**: From ilmenite preprocessing

**Other Minerals:**
- **Troilite (FeS)**: ~1% of mare basalt; ubiquitous in meteorites; sulfur source
- **Apatite (Ca5(PO4)3(OH,F,Cl))**: Very rare; REE source; phosphorus source
- **Monazite ((Ce,La,Nd,Th)PO4)**: REE source
- **Yttrobetafite ((Ca,Y,U,Th,Pb,REE)2(Ti,Nb)2O2)**: Yttrium source
- **Zircon (ZrSiO4)**: From rare granite sources
- **Cristobalite (SiO2)**: ~6% in mare basalt; quartz polymorph

**KREEP Materials:**
- Enriched in: K (K2O), Rare Earth Elements, P (P2O5), Th, U
- Limited to Procellarum KREEP Terrane
- Concentrations: K2O (0.8%), P2O5 (0.6%), Nd (460 ppm), Sm (127 ppm)

### 3. METEORITIC MATERIALS

**Iron-Nickel Meteorites:**
- **Kamacite**: 90-95% Fe, 5-10% Ni; contaminated with ~0.5% Co
- **Taenite**: 35-80% Fe, 20-65% Ni
- **Generic NiFe**: 4-30% Ni content; subsurface deposits at mascon locations
- **Tungsten inclusions**: 0.1-5 μg/g in NiFe alloys; 0.3 μg/g in highland rock

**Other Meteoritic Components:**
- Cobalt: Up to 9% in some NiFe meteorites
- Troilite (FeS): Common in meteorites
- Selenium: ~25 ppm in stony and carbonaceous meteorites; 0.5-10 ppm in chondrites
- Carbonaceous material: ~47% in some asteroid impacts (Yutu-2 samples)

**Nanophase Metallic Iron:**
- 10 nm diameter inclusions in regolith grains
- 20-25% metallic iron from impact heating
- High magnetic susceptibility

### 4. VOLATILE RESOURCES

**Solar Wind Implanted (upper 2-3m regolith):**
- **Hydrogen**: 50-150 μg/g (96% of solar wind composition); ~120 ppm; ~3 × 10^-4 by mass
- **Helium-4**: 14-100 μg/g; ~4% of implants
- **Helium-3**: 4-30 ng/g; enriched 100x in high-TiO2 deposits; ~20 ppb in mature ilmenite
- **Carbon**: 124-300 μg/g
- **Nitrogen**: 81-150 μg/g
- **Noble gases**: Argon (3.1×10^-4 cm³ STP/g for ³⁶Ar)

**Volatile Compounds (released at 600-700°C):**
- Water (H2O)
- Carbon monoxide (CO)
- Carbon dioxide (CO2)
- Methane (CH4)
- Nitrogen (N2)
- Ammonia (NH3): 0.3% in Cabeus
- Hydrogen sulfide (H2S)
- Sulfur dioxide (SO2)
- Hydrogen fluoride (HF)
- Hydrogen chloride (HCl)
- Fluorine (F2), Chlorine (Cl2)

**Water Ice Deposits:**
- **Lunar South Pole**: 6.6 million metric tons
- **Cabeus Crater**: 5.6 ± 2.9% water concentration
- **Permanently Shadowed Regions (PSR)**: Variable concentrations
- **LCROSS Impact Analysis**: 5.50% water/ice, 5.70% CO, 1.40% H2, 0.92% H2S, 0.48% Hg, 0.33% NH3

**Volatile Enrichment in Ilmenite:**
- 20-200 ppm volatiles
- Degree increases at finer grain sizes (~10 µm)
- ~1-100 ppm typical (C, N, S); higher in permanently shadowed craters

## PROCESSED RESOURCES

### 1. PURE ELEMENTS (Metals & Non-metals)

**Major Metals:**
- **Oxygen (O2)**: From FFC anode evolution, H2 reduction, water electrolysis, carbothermal reduction; 1-5% extraction (H2 reduction), 15-20% (carbothermal), >99% (FFC); production rates: 250-660 kg/year demonstrated
- **Iron (Fe)**: Wrought iron, pure iron; from ilmenite, magnetite, NiFe meteorites; via H2 reduction at 900-1000°C, FFC process, Mond carbonyl process, magnetic separation
- **Aluminum (Al)**: >99% pure via FFC; from anorthite via HCl weathering, carbothermal reduction at 1650°C, electrolysis
- **Silicon (Si)**: Metallurgical grade 99%, solar grade 99.9999%; from SiO2 via FFC, molten salt electrolysis, carbothermal reduction, vapor phase pyrolysis at 1250°C
- **Titanium (Ti)**: >99% pure from rutile via FFC Cambridge at 900-1100°C
- **Calcium (Ca)**: From CaO reduction; twice as conductive as Cu
- **Magnesium (Mg)**: From MgO reduction above 650°C; from olivine aluminothermic reduction at 1000°C

**Minor/Trace Metals:**
- **Nickel (Ni)**: From NiFe meteorites via Mond process (Ni(CO)4 at 55°C/1 bar)
- **Cobalt (Co)**: From meteorites via Mond process (Co2(CO)8 at 150°C/35 bar); roasting with S then electrochemical extraction
- **Tungsten (W)**: From meteorite inclusions, lunar ores; via W(CO)6 decomposition; melting point 3422°C; density 19.3 g/cm³
- **Selenium (Se)**: From meteorite material, troilite processing; electrolytic refining; bandgap 1.99 eV (p-type semiconductor)
- **Copper (Cu)**: From electrolysis and trace extraction
- **Chromium (Cr)**: From chromite and olivine
- **Sodium (Na)**: From weathering and KREEP regions
- **Potassium (K)**: From orthoclase and KREEP

**Non-metals:**
- **Hydrogen (H2)**: From water electrolysis, H2 reduction recycling, volatile extraction
- **Carbon (C)**: From Sabatier reaction recycling, methane pyrolysis at 1400°C, Boudouard reaction at 700°C, Bosch reaction at 530-730°C

### 2. METAL OXIDES & COMPOUNDS

**Major Oxides:**
- **Silicon Dioxide (SiO2)**: 44.9% of regolith; from HCl weathering, precipitation from silicic acid; fused silica glass at 1700°C
- **Aluminum Oxide (Al2O3)**: 17.5% of regolith; from HCl acid leaching, calcination at 400°C; melting point 2135°C
- **Iron Oxides**: FeO (12.2%), Fe2O3 (hematite), Fe3O4 (magnetite); from oxidation, ilmenite processing
- **Calcium Oxide (CaO)**: 12.1% of regolith; from anorthite reduction, limestone calcination
- **Magnesium Oxide (MgO)**: 8.8% of regolith; from forsterite reduction, gibbsite intermediate
- **Titanium Dioxide (TiO2)**: 2.5% of regolith (up to 7% in high-Ti mare); rutile from ilmenite H2 reduction at 1000°C
- **Sodium Oxide (Na2O)**: 0.44%
- **Chromium Oxide (Cr2O3)**: 0.27%
- **Potassium Oxide (K2O)**: 0.11%

**Minor Oxides:**
- Manganese oxides (MnO)
- Sulfur oxides (SO2, SO3)
- Nitrogen oxides (NO, NO2)
- Phosphorus pentoxide (P2O5)

### 3. METAL ALLOYS

**Structural Steels:**
- **Tool Steel**: Fe + <2% C + 9-18% W; for cutting tools
- **Silicon Electrical Steel**: Fe + up to 3% Si; magnetic capacity 1.6 T; for electromagnets/motors
- **Carbon Steel**: Fe + <2% C
- **Ferrotitanium**: 45-75% Ti; oxygen getter, pyrotechnic fuel

**Lightweight Alloys:**
- **Magnalium**: Al + Mg; lightweight, corrosion-resistant
- **Silumin**: 3-25% Si in Al (87% Al, 13% Si typical); high durability for pistons
- **Al-Si Alloys**: 20-50% Si; for welds, brazing
- **MgSi Alloy**: General purpose structures; added to Al for high-strength
- **AlCaSi Alloy**: From anorthite reduction; general purpose

**High-Performance Alloys:**
- **Titanium Aluminide (TiAl)**: Excellent high-temperature properties
- **Ti6Al4V**: 6% Al, 4% V; aerospace alloy
- **NiTi (Nitinol)**: Shape memory alloy; near-equiatomic; artificial muscle actuators

**Magnetic Alloys:**
- **Permalloy**: 20% Fe + 80% Ni (or 45/55); high magnetic permeability for shielding
- **Supermalloy**: 79% Ni, 16% Fe, 5% Mo; very high permeability
- **Silicon Steel**: Fe + 3% Si; for motor cores and electromagnets

**Thermal/Electrical Alloys:**
- **Kovar (Fernico)**: 53.5% Fe, 29% Ni, 17% Co, 0.3% Mn, 0.2% Si, <0.01% C; high electrical/thermal conductivity
- **Invar**: 64% Fe, 36% Ni; low thermal expansion
- **Inovco**: 62.5% Fe, 33% Ni, 4.5% Co; very low thermal expansion

**Permanent Magnets:**
- **AlNiCo**: 8-12% Al, 15-26% Ni, 5-24% Co, minor Cu, minor Ti; 35% Fe, 35% Co, 15% Ni, 7% Al, 4% Cu, 4% Ti (specific composition); usable to 540°C; Curie temp 850°C
- **NdFeB (Neodymium)**: 30% Nd, 66.8% Fe, 1.0% B, 0.4% Al, 0.8% Nb, 1% Dy; rare earth bonded magnets
- **Ferrite Magnets**: Fe2O3/Fe2O3·CoO; cobalt ferrite (CoFe2O4); BaFe12O19 (hard ferrite); NiFe2O4 (soft ferrite)
- **Iron-Cobalt**: High induction saturation 2.2 T

**Superconducting Alloys:**
- **NbTi**: Niobium-titanium superconductors

**Meteoritic Alloys:**
- **FeTi Amalgam/Sponge**: From ilmenite; TiFe, TiFe2; 90% oxygen recovery

## PRODUCTION METHODS MENTIONED

### Pyrometallurgical:
- **FFC (Fray-Farthing-Chen) / Metalysis Process**: Electrochemical reduction at 900-1100°C in molten CaCl2; yields >99% pure metals (Ti, Fe, Al, Mg, Si) in solid state; >99% oxygen extraction at anode
- **Hydrogen Reduction**: Heating regolith/ilmenite with H2 at 700-1000°C; FeTiO3 + H2 → Fe + TiO2 + H2O at 900°C; 1-5% oxygen extraction
- **Carbothermal Reduction**: Methane cracking at ~1625°C; 15-20% oxygen extraction; high-temperature mineral processing with carbon
- **Molten Oxide Electrolysis (MRE)**: 20-30 kg O2 per 100 kg regolith; liquid metal at cathode; evaporated oxides removed
- **Solar Concentrators**: Fresnel lenses, parabolic mirrors to 900-2700°C for smelting
- **Aluminothermic Reduction**: Of olivine at 1000°C
- **Sintering**: Regolith at 1100°C with laser or solar concentrators
- **Cast Basalt**: Melting at 1180-1240°C, cooling in sand molds
- **Vapor Phase Pyrolysis**: SiO2 → Si at 1250°C in vacuum

### Hydrometallurgical:
- **HCl Artificial Weathering**: Acid leaching of anorthite, orthoclase, pyroxene at room temp for Al, Si, Ca extraction
- **Fractional Distillation**: For volatile separation; trichlorosilane for solar-grade Si
- **Electrolysis**: Water splitting via PEM; H2O → H2 + ½O2
- **Mond Process**: Carbonyl processing at 40-80°C for Ni, Fe, Co, W separation via metal carbonyls
- **Oxidation**: Fayalite to magnetite at 400-700°C; Fe to Fe2O3/Fe3O4
- **Thermal Extraction**: Volatiles at 600-700°C from regolith surface; 75% recovery
- **Hydrothermal Synthesis**: Quartz crystal growth <573°C in autoclave

### Chemical Synthesis:
- **Sabatier Reaction**: CO2 + 4H2 → CH4 + 2H2O at 300°C
- **Boudouard Reaction**: 2CO → C + CO2 at 700°C
- **Bosch Reaction**: CO2 + 2H2 → C + 2H2O at 530-730°C with Fe catalyst
- **Haber-Bosch Process**: N2 + 3H2 → 2NH3 with Fe catalyst
- **Solvay Process**: NaCl + CaCO3 ↔ Na2CO3 + CaCl2
- **Rochow Process**: 2CH3Cl + Si → (CH3)2SiCl2; hydrolysis to PDMS
- **Steam Reforming**: CH4 + H2O → CO + 3H2 at 850°C with Ni catalyst
- **Methanol Synthesis**: CO + 2H2 → CH3OH at 250°C, 5-10 MPa with Al2O3 catalyst
- **Ostwald/Contact Process**: SO2 → SO3 → H2SO4

### Physical Separation:
- **Magnetic Separation**: Ilmenite beneficiation; nanophase Fe extraction; electromagnetic separation of Ni, Co
- **Beneficiation**: Mineral concentration via magnetic, density, or size separation
- **Froth Flotation**: W microparticle separation after grinding

### Advanced Manufacturing:
- **3D Printing / Additive Manufacturing**: SLS (selective laser sintering), SLM (selective laser melting), EBF3 (electron beam freeform fabrication), FDM (fused deposition modeling)
- **Czochralski Process**: Single-crystal Si growth at 1500°C; sapphire synthesis
- **Verneuil Flame Process**: Sapphire/ruby synthesis
- **Photolithography**: Copper wire patterns for motors/circuits
- **Sand Casting**: With bentonite binder
- **Hot Isostatic Pressing**: With silicone oil medium
